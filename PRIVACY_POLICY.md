# Privacy Policy for GitHub Fork Sync

**Last Updated:** May 1, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how GitHub Fork Sync ("the Extension") collects, uses, and protects your information.

### 1. Information We Collect
To provide its core functionality, the Extension collects the following data:
*   **Authentication Information:** We collect your GitHub Personal Access Token (PAT).
*   **Personally Identifiable Information (Future):** We plan to collect your email address if you opt-in to our background automated sync (cron job) feature to receive status reports.
*   **Website Content:** The Extension reads the text and links specifically on your `github.com` repositories page to identify forked repositories and inject the syncing user interface.

### 2. How We Use Your Information
*   Your **GitHub PAT** is used exclusively to authenticate requests to the official GitHub API (`api.github.com`) to check commit differences and trigger upstream merges.
*   Your **Email Address** will be used exclusively to send you automated reports regarding the status of your scheduled fork syncs.
*   **Website Content** is read instantly in your browser to render the extension's user interface. This data is never transmitted to us.

### 3. Data Storage and Security
*   Your GitHub PAT is encrypted and stored locally on your device within your browser's secure `chrome.storage.local`. It is never transmitted to any third-party servers other than the official GitHub API.
*   We do not store your data on our own remote database servers. All operations happen peer-to-peer between your browser and the respective APIs.

### 4. Data Sharing and Disclosure
We do not sell, rent, or transfer your personal data to any third parties. Your data is used strictly for the single purpose of synchronizing your GitHub repositories and providing you with status updates. We do not use your data for advertising, creditworthiness, or lending purposes.

### 5. User Consent and Revocation
By installing the Extension and providing your token or email, you consent to the practices described in this policy. You can revoke access at any time by:
1. Deleting your PAT from the extension's popup.
2. Uninstalling the extension.
3. Revoking the token directly from your GitHub Developer Settings.
